For the ”Machine Learning for Graphs” course I developed a
novel algorithm to perform non-linear (RNN-based) causal discovery between random variables (such as climate indices), building on the [PCMCI method](https://www.science.org/doi/full/10.1126/sciadv.aau4996), as an extension of a [study on regime-dependent causality](http://pubs.aip.org/aip/cha/article/30/11/113115/595926).

## Abstract
After introducing the field of causal discovery and its core concepts, I provide an overview of the state of the art of causal discovery, addressing how the latter can be achieved in systems with causal dynamics that evolve over time.
I proceed by reproducing a recent study in the field, which focuses on alternating linear causal regimes. This research is then extended by introducing a new algorithm for causal discovery, MCI-RNN, which uses temporal networks to represent the non-linear causal links between time-dependent variables.
